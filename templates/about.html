Our Mission

The National 4-H Geospatial Leadership Team is dedicated to empowering young leaders to engage in meaningful research and public service projects that tackle real-world challenges across the nation.

Through cutting-edge training in GIS (Geographic Information Systems) and GPS (Global Positioning Systems), we equip our members with the skills to drive impactful change in their communities.

By fostering collaboration with educational institutions, government agencies, and community organizations, we inspire youth to become leaders in data-driven decision-making, environmental sustainability, and community development.

What Do We Do?

The National 4-H Geospatial Team is a powerhouse of youth-driven innovation, empowering students to explore geospatial technology while making a tangible impact in their communities. Our team engages in a wide range of activities that build leadership, technical expertise, and real-world problem-solving skills.

  • Workshops & Training: Teaching GIS and spatial thinking through hands-on learning experiences.
  • Weekly Check-ins: Covering project briefings, geospatial training, and Career Nights featuring industry professionals, educators and government officials.
  • Individual Research Projects: Youth-led studies in fields like agriculture, environmental science, and public health, using GIS to analyze real-world data.
  • Conferences: Presenting our work at major events like the Esri User Conference, NC State Extension, and national STEM summits.
  • Community Engagement: Partnering with local organizations and schools to introduce GIS to youth and educators.
  • Expanding Our Portfolio: Documenting our success through StoryMaps, ArcGIS Hub sites, and national impact reports.
  • Industry Collaboration: Working with partners like Esri, NC State University, and Google to expand opportunities for youth in geospatial fields.

Whether you're a student, educator, or industry professional, there's a place for you to learn, engage, and contribute with the National 4-H Geospatial Team. Join us in shaping the future of GIS!
