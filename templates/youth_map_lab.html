Youth Map Lab

AI + GIS Workshops for Curious Minds & Change-Makers

Featured Workshop

🌾 Satellites to Seeds

Students use satellite imagery, AI tools, and GIS to plan the farms of the future. Activities include smart farm design, crop zone mapping, sustainability analysis, and civic storytelling.

What Students Will Do

  • Think like scientists. Ask bold questions and investigate real-world challenges using data and spatial reasoning.
  • Use real tools. Explore AI, ArcGIS, Google Earth, and satellite imagery — no experience needed.
  • Make cool stuff. Build smart farm maps or "time machines" that show how neighborhoods change over time.
  • Tell their story. Present like civic tech leaders — using maps, visuals, and evidence to make change.
  • Decode local history. Use AI to read old photos, deeds, and articles — connecting data to community memory.
  • Design for the future. Apply sustainability and equity to projects that impact real places and people.

Who It's For

  • Middle and high school students (Grades 5–9) — curious, creative, and ready to explore
  • Schools, libraries, after-school programs, equity clubs, and STEM camps
  • No prior experience needed — just imagination and a willingness to learn by doing

We Bring Everything

📚 Complete Workshop Package

Facilitators. Lessons. Maps. AI demos. Paper kits. All you need is a space, basic AV access, and a group of students ready to explore.
